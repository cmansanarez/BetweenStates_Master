# XR Final Assignment Specification
### Course: CRCP – Mobile Computing and Augmented Reality

---

## OVERVIEW

The final assignment is divided into two main parts:

1. Conceptual Advancement (70%)
2. Continued Developmental Effort (30%)

The goal is to present yourself as a **budding XR entrepreneur** pitching to:
- venture capitalists
- angel investors
- government and NGO grant managers

You must demonstrate:
- a compelling XR concept
- feasibility and real-world application
- early-stage prototype development

---

## PART 1: CONCEPTUAL ADVANCEMENT (70%)

### Premise

You are pitching an XR concept that:
- benefits society and/or creates revenue opportunities
- is already in development
- demonstrates leadership, clarity, and execution

Your goal:
Create a **professional, well-organized conceptual XR package** that convinces funders to support your idea.

---

## CORE EVALUATION CRITERIA

Your presentation (or site) must demonstrate:

### 1. Professionalism
- polished presentation
- well-organized content
- credible concept supported by research

### 2. Cohesion
- consistent design system
- unified visual identity
- formatting that feels like a real company or studio

### 3. XR-Specific Thinking
- clear use of XR capabilities
- not just a traditional mobile app
- must demonstrate spatial, embodied, and immersive qualities

### 4. Emphasis on 3D / Spatial Nature
- show depth, layering, field of view
- exploded views or diagrams
- spatial relationships between elements

### 5. Visual Communication
- prioritize visuals over text
- diagrams, storyboards, and renderings
- avoid text-heavy slides

---

## REQUIRED COMPONENTS

---

### 1. Persona Section (50 pts)

Define your audience clearly.

#### Primary Persona (70–75% focus)
Include:
- demographics
- goals
- behaviors
- pain points

#### Secondary Persona (25–30% focus)
- supporting stakeholder (e.g. educator, organizer)

#### Notes
- do NOT create persona for investors
- focus on users of the experience

---

### 2. Improved Storyboard (50 pts)

- clearly show experience progression
- strong storytelling
- cohesive visual style
- easy to read and understand

---

### 3. Roadmap (50 pts)

Create a **12-month development plan** that includes:

- phases of development
- team size at each stage
- key milestones
- high-level funding allocation ($1M)

---

### 4. Information Architecture (50 pts)

Create a **system blueprint** showing:

- devices (phones, headsets, etc.)
- software layers
- cloud/network components
- user roles and positions

---

### 5. Marketing Visual (50 pts)

Create one high-impact image that:

- represents the full concept
- shows depth and spatial awareness
- includes callouts / annotations
- would attract investor attention at a conference

---

### 6. Bibliography (25 pts)

Include:
- at least 5 non-AI sources
- at least 3 AI citations

Must:
- reference sources within the project
- follow proper citation practices

---

### PRESENTATION QUALITY (50 pts)

You will be evaluated on:

- clarity of communication
- confidence and delivery
- ability to explain concept and system
- overall professionalism

---

## ADDITIONAL NOTES

- Do not overcrowd slides/pages
- Maintain strong visual design
- Use padding, spacing, and typography intentionally
- Aim for a polished, professional look
- This should feel like a real pitch or portfolio piece

---

## PART 2: DEVELOPMENTAL EFFORT (30%)

You must implement at least:

- 5 functional features
  - at least 4 basic features
  - at least 1 advanced feature

---

### BASIC FEATURE OPTIONS

Examples include:

- display images or 3D models
- modify textures or materials
- play audio
- animate movement
- create buttons
- timed triggers
- orbiting objects
- object translation (XY/Z)
- open external links
- object interactions (chase, target)
- visibility toggles
- scaling
- rotation/spin
- animation playback
- bounce physics

---

### ADVANCED FEATURE OPTIONS (Choose at least one)

- QR code anchoring
- object recognition (non-QR)
- lighting changes
- AI integration
- face filter / mask
- multi-layer scenes
- IoT data integration
- live video embedding
- GPS directional pointer

---

## DEVELOPMENT SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS

### 1. Environment Design (25 pts)
- background or environment that matches concept

---

### 2. Dev Process Video (50 pts)
- 2–5 minute walkthrough
- show tools and setup
- explain logic and triggers
- discuss challenges

---

### 3. Feature Demo (100 pts)
- demonstrate at least 5 features
- include annotations or explanation

---

### 4. Functionality Quality (25 pts)
- stability and usability of features

---

## IMPORTANT NOTES

- Show your work clearly
- You may submit multiple videos if needed
- Focus on demonstrating your process and thinking

---

## FINAL GOAL

Deliver a project that:

- demonstrates XR understanding
- shows technical capability
- communicates a compelling idea
- feels like a real-world, fundable concept
